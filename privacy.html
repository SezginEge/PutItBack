Put It Back Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 14, 2026

This policy describes how the current Put It Back iOS app handles information in connection with focus sessions, analytics, and purchases.

1. Information the app uses

The current iOS app uses the following categories of information:

  • Motion data while a focus session is active, so the app can detect when the phone is picked up.
  • Local session history, including session start and end times, duration, pickup count, selected voice pack, completion status, streak values, and the last spoken quote.
  • Preference data, such as the selected voice pack and preferred session duration.
  • Purchase state related to the one-time unlock for paid voice packs.
  • Usage analytics events such as app opens, session starts, pickups, session completion, voice pack selection, paywall views, purchases, and share activity.

2. How the information is used

Put It Back uses this information to:

  • run the focus timer and detect pickups during active sessions;
  • restore interrupted sessions and show remaining time;
  • display analytics, streaks, and result summaries inside the app;
  • enable and restore paid voice pack access through the App Store; and
  • understand product usage through analytics events.

3. Analytics and third-party services

The current iOS app code includes Firebase Analytics. Firebase Analytics is used for product and behavior events such as app opens, session starts, pickups, paywall views, purchases, and sharing actions. Your use of the app may therefore involve processing by Google Firebase under their applicable terms and privacy materials.

4. Purchases

Paid voice packs are handled through Apple's StoreKit and the App Store. Put It Back receives purchase and entitlement information needed to unlock paid content and restore that content when available. Payment details are handled by Apple, not entered directly into the app.

5. Local storage

Session history and session recovery details are stored locally on the device so the app can show analytics, remember preferences, and recover an active session. This includes session duration, selected voice pack, pickup count, streak information, and related result data.

6. Data sharing

Based on the current app implementation reviewed for this site, information may be shared with service providers involved in analytics and purchases, specifically Firebase Analytics and Apple. This page does not promise uses beyond the behavior visible in the current iOS codebase.

7. Your choices

  • You can decline motion access, but pickup detection will not function as intended.
  • You can manage app permissions through iOS Settings.
  • You can manage App Store purchases and restore transactions through Apple services and in-app restore flows where available.

8. Policy updates

This privacy page should be updated if the app's analytics stack, storage behavior, or purchase flows change. If those behaviors change materially, the website and app disclosure should be revised to match.

9. Contact

For privacy questions, use the support contact that accompanies the app distribution or website deployment for Put It Back.
